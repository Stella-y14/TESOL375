Profile of an Excellent Teacher

TESOL 375 Capstone | Fall 2025

This profile is not just a collection of observations, but a declaration of the practices I am committed to implementing in my own classroom. It defines who I am as a teacher today.

1. Participatory Learning

Key Question: How do you facilitate language learning through active student engagement?

The Learner as a Doer

I believe in moving beyond passive listening to active engagement. My observations have confirmed that the most effective learning occurs when students shift from listeners to active participants.

Peer Interaction

Collaboration is key. I implement pair and group work to allow students to negotiate meaning, provide peer feedback, and deepen their understanding of concepts like topic sentences.

2. Comprehensible Input

Key Question: How do you make lesson objectives, content & instructions more comprehensible?

Scaffolding with Meta-Language

I use relatable metaphors, such as the "Hamburger Structure" (Topic, Support, Conclusion), to provide a clear mental model for complex writing tasks. Explicit phrases like "Use the language of the prompt" serve as hooks for memory.

Attending Behaviors

Non-verbal communication—eye contact, nodding, and gestures—signals to students that they are "seen." This reduces anxiety and creates a safe space for input to be received and processed.

3. Assessing Learning

Key Question: How does assessment inform your instruction?

Continuous Monitoring

Assessment isn't just a final exam. I allocate approximately 8 minutes per class for check-ins like "Okay?" or "Questions?" to catch misunderstandings early.

Culture of Error

I strive to correct gently (e.g., blaming the font size, not the student) to maintain confidence. In my classroom, errors are welcomed as necessary steps in learning.

Feedback Priority

During discussion phases, I prioritize fluency over accuracy to ensure the flow of communication isn't interrupted.

4. Self-Directed Learning

Key Question: How do you motivate students to be more self-directed learners?

Fostering Autonomy

Motivation thrives on relevance. By allowing students to choose their own sources and arguments, I shift the ownership of learning from the teacher to the student.

Goal Setting

My role is to help students set realistic goals and recognize specific skills for improvement, sustaining their motivation even through difficult tasks.

My Implementation Resolutions

Interact Prioritizing peer-to-peer negotiation.
Scaffold Using visual metaphors & meta-language.
Connect Using names & cues to build connection.
Assess Frequent, low-stakes checks.
